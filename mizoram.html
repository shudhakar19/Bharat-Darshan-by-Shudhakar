Mizoram

"Peace Pays"

Quick Facts

Capital: Aizawl
Chief Minister: Lalduhoma
Governor: Kambhampati Hari Babu
Official Language: Mizo, English
Population: ~12 lakh
Area: 21,081 sq km
Literacy Rate: 91.33%
State Animal: Serow
State Bird: Mrs. Hume's Pheasant
State Flower: Red Vanda

Popular Tourist Attractions

Reiek Tlang

Popular hilltop viewpoint offering stunning views and cultural village experiences.

Lunglei

A scenic town ideal for eco-tourism and offbeat exploration.

Vantawng Falls

One of the highest waterfalls in Mizoram, nestled in dense forests.

Nature & Wildlife

Mizoram is home to tropical forests and wildlife sanctuaries like Dampa Tiger Reserve and Lengteng Wildlife Sanctuary.

Famous Personalities

  • Lal Thanhawla: Veteran politician and former CM.
  • R. Lalzirliana: Prominent political figure in state governance.
  • Vanlalzawma: Former MP and known voice of Mizo culture and rights.

Local Handicrafts

Mizoram is renowned for hand-woven shawls, bamboo baskets, cane furniture, and colorful traditional textiles.

Climate

The climate is moderate and pleasant year-round, with monsoon rains and cool winters due to its elevation and forest cover.

Culture & Festivals

Festivals

Chapchar Kut is the most famous festival, celebrating the cutting of bamboo forests for jhum farming.

Dance & Music

Cheraw (bamboo dance) is iconic, with vibrant music and community participation.

Cuisine

Popular dishes include Bai, Bamboo Shoot Curry, and Arsa Buhchiar – a flavorful rice and meat combo.

Clothing

Traditional Mizo outfits include Puan (woven wraparounds) worn during cultural events and ceremonies.

Did You Know? Mizoram means “Land of the Highlanders” and is one of India’s most literate states.
