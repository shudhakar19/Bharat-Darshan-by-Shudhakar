Nagaland

"Land of Festivals"

Quick Facts

Capital: Kohima
Chief Minister: Neiphiu Rio
Governor: La. Ganesan
Official Language: English
Population: ~22 lakh
Area: 16,579 sq km
Literacy Rate: 80.11%
State Animal: Mithun
State Bird: Blyth's Tragopan
State Flower: Rhododendron

Popular Tourist Attractions

Hornbill Festival

A cultural extravaganza held in Kisama Heritage Village showcasing tribal diversity.

Dzukou Valley

Famous for its seasonal flowers and breathtaking trekking trails.

Kohima War Cemetery

A serene memorial site for WWII soldiers with panoramic views.

Nature & Wildlife

Nagaland houses diverse fauna in Intanki National Park and various forest reserves teeming with birdlife and wildlife.

Famous Personalities

  • Neiphiu Rio: Prominent CM and advocate for peace and development.
  • Theja Meru: Artist and promoter of Naga music and culture.
  • Temsula Ao: Celebrated poet and short story writer.

Local Handicrafts

Nagaland is known for tribal bamboo crafts, hand-woven shawls, wood carvings, and colorful beadwork.

Climate

Nagaland experiences a moderate climate with cool winters and monsoon rains from June to September.

Culture & Festivals

Festivals

The Hornbill Festival brings together all tribes to celebrate heritage, dance, and music.

Traditional Attire

Tribal war dresses with feathers, beads, and bold colors are worn during festivals.

Food

Naga cuisine includes smoked meat, fermented bamboo shoots, and rice beer.
