Arunachal Pradesh

"The Land of the Rising Sun"

Quick Facts

Capital: Itanagar
Chief Minister: Pema Khandu
Governor: Kaiwalya Trivikram Parnaik
Official Languages: English
Population: ~15 lakh
Area: 83,743 sq km
Literacy Rate: 66.95%
State Animal: Mithun
State Bird: Great Hornbill
State Flower: Foxtail Orchid

Popular Tourist Attractions

Tawang Monastery

The largest monastery in India, surrounded by Himalayan peaks.

Ziro Valley

UNESCO heritage site known for its music festivals and tribal lifestyle.

Namdapha

A biodiversity hotspot, home to snow leopards and red pandas.

Nature & Wildlife

The state is blessed with pristine forests, rare orchids, and endangered wildlife like the red panda and clouded leopard. National parks like Namdapha and Pakhui are ecotourism hotspots.

Famous Personalities

  • Pema Khandu: Long-serving Chief Minister of Arunachal Pradesh.
  • Mamang Dai: Renowned journalist and Padma Shri awardee.
  • Tapi Mra: First Arunachali to scale Mount Everest.

Local Handicrafts

Tribal craftsmanship includes bamboo work, wood carvings, hand-woven woolen shawls, and intricately beaded jewelry. Each tribe has its unique art form and attire.

Climate

Mountainous regions experience cold winters and pleasant summers. The state receives heavy rainfall and remains lush throughout the year.

Culture & Festivals

Festivals

Losar, Nyokum, and Dree are celebrated with tribal dances and rituals.

Dances & Music

Each tribe has vibrant folk dances performed in colorful attire.

Cuisine

Dishes like bamboo shoot curry, Thukpa, and smoked pork are common.

Clothing

Colorful traditional attire made of handwoven wool, with headgear and ornaments.

Did You Know? Arunachal shares international borders with three countries: China, Bhutan, and Myanmar.
