Gujarat

"Jewel of the West"

Quick Facts

Capital: Gandhinagar
Chief Minister: Bhupendrabhai Patel
Governor: Acharya Devvrat
Official Language: Gujarati
Population: ~6.27 crore
Area: 196,024 sq km
Literacy Rate: 82%
State Animal: Asiatic Lion
State Bird: Greater Flamingo
State Flower: Marigold

Popular Tourist Attractions

Statue of Unity

World's tallest statue honoring Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.

Gir National Park

Home of the last remaining Asiatic Lions in the wild.

Rann of Kutch

White salt desert famous for the Rann Utsav and desert beauty.

Nature & Wildlife

Gujarat boasts diverse landscapes — from Gir forests with lions to flamingos in the Rann. Wildlife sanctuaries include Velavadar, Marine National Park, and more.

Famous Personalities

  • Mahatma Gandhi: Father of the Nation, born in Porbandar.
  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel: Iron Man of India and unifier of the nation.
  • Vikram Sarabhai: Father of India’s space program.

Local Handicrafts

Gujarat is known for Bandhani textiles, mirror work, pottery, Patola sarees, wood carving, and embroidery from Kutch and Saurashtra.

Climate

Gujarat has a semi-arid climate with hot summers, short monsoons, and mild winters. The desert regions can be extreme but beautiful.

Culture & Festivals

Festivals

Navratri is celebrated with Garba and Dandiya, unmatched in color and energy.

Dance & Music

Folk forms like Ras and Garba dominate cultural events.

Cuisine

Gujarati Thali, Dhokla, Khandvi, and Thepla are must-tries.

Clothing

Colorful Chaniya-Cholis, Bandhani sarees, and Kediyu for men are traditional favorites.

Did You Know? Gujarat has the longest coastline of any Indian state and is one of the most industrialized states in India.
