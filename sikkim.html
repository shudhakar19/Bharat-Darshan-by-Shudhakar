Sikkim

"The Land of Hidden Paradise"

Quick Facts

Capital: Gangtok
Chief Minister: Prem Singh Tamang
Governor: Ganga Prasad
Official Language: Nepali
Population: ~6.1 lakh
Area: 7,096 sq km
Literacy Rate: 81.42%
State Animal: Red Panda
State Bird: Himalayan Monal
State Flower: Nobile Orchid

Popular Tourist Attractions

Tsomgo Lake

A high-altitude lake surrounded by snow-capped peaks.

Nathula Pass

A historic mountain pass, known for its scenic views and trade route to China.

Rumtek Monastery

A key Buddhist monastery with serene surroundings.

Nature & Wildlife

Sikkim is known for its rich biodiversity, with famous wildlife sanctuaries like Khangchendzonga National Park, home to the rare Red Panda and snow leopards.

Famous Personalities

  • Padma Bhushan B B Lal: Renowned archaeologist.
  • Pawan Chamling: Former Chief Minister of Sikkim.
  • Bhanu A: Nepali poet and writer.

Local Handicrafts

Sikkim is famous for its beautiful hand-woven carpets, wooden handicrafts, and traditional metalwork.

Climate

The state experiences a subtropical highland climate with heavy monsoon rains and cold winters. The weather is generally cool throughout the year.

Culture & Festivals

Dance

Buddhist dances like the Cham dance are a significant part of Sikkimese culture.

Festivals

Lhosar (Nepali New Year) and Buddha Jayanti are among the most celebrated festivals.

Food

The cuisine includes momo, gundruk, and thukpa.

Attire

Traditional Sikkimese attire includes the 'bakhu' worn by both men and women.

Did You Know? Sikkim was the first state in India to introduce organic farming on a state-wide scale.
