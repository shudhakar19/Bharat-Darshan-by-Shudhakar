Puducherry

"The French Riviera of the East"

Quick Facts

Capital: Puducherry
Lieutenant Governor: Tamilisai Soundararajan
Chief Minister: N. Rangasamy
Official Languages: Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, English, French
Population: ~1.25 million
Area: 492 sq km
Literacy Rate: 86.5%
State Animal: Indian Palm Squirrel
State Bird: Asian Koel
State Flower: Cannonball Flower

Popular Tourist Attractions

Rock Beach

A scenic promenade perfect for sunrise walks.

Auroville

A unique universal township promoting unity and peace.

Sri Aurobindo Ashram

A spiritual retreat founded by Sri Aurobindo and The Mother.

Nature & Wildlife

Puducherry is home to coastal lagoons, backwaters, and mangrove forests. Ousteri Lake is a haven for migratory birds.

Famous Personalities

  • Sri Aurobindo: Philosopher, poet, and spiritual leader.
  • The Mother (Mirra Alfassa): Spiritual collaborator of Sri Aurobindo and founder of Auroville.

Local Handicrafts

Pottery, hand-painted tiles, papier-mâché crafts, and handmade candles are popular in Puducherry’s artisan markets.

Climate

Tropical wet and dry climate with hot summers and pleasant winters. Monsoons arrive from October to December.

Culture & Festivals

Architecture

Colonial French quarters with vibrant mustard-yellow buildings and bougainvillea-covered walls.

Festivals

Pongal, Bastille Day, and Aurobindo’s birth anniversary are widely celebrated.

Food

Fusion cuisine combining Tamil spices with French techniques—try ratatouille with idli!

Did You Know? Puducherry celebrates Bastille Day with a French-style parade and Tamil-French cultural fusion!
