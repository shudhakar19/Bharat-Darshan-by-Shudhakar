Jammu and Kashmir

"Paradise on Earth"

Quick Facts

Capital: Srinagar (Summer), Jammu (Winter)
Lieutenant Governor: Manoj Sinha
Official Languages: Urdu, Kashmiri, Dogri, Hindi, English
Population: ~1.25 crore
Area: 55,538 sq km
Literacy Rate: 67.2%
State Animal: Hangul
State Bird: Black-necked Crane
State Flower: Lotus

Popular Tourist Attractions

Dal Lake

Picturesque lake famous for shikara rides and floating gardens.

Vaishno Devi

One of the holiest pilgrimage sites in India.

Gulmarg

Popular for skiing, meadows, and snow-clad mountains.

Nature & Wildlife

Home to Dachigam National Park, Hemis National Park, and rare species like the Hangul and Snow Leopard.

Famous Personalities

  • Sheikh Abdullah: Known as the "Lion of Kashmir".
  • Jigmet Wangchuk: Conservationist and cultural ambassador.
  • Begum Akbar Jahan: Prominent politician and social worker.

Local Handicrafts

Renowned for Pashmina shawls, papier-mâché crafts, Kashmiri carpets, and walnut wood carvings.

Climate

Temperate climate in Kashmir Valley with snow in winters; sub-tropical in Jammu with hot summers and mild winters.

Culture & Festivals

Festivals

Celebrates Eid, Baisakhi, Navroz, and the famous Tulip Festival.

Food

Popular dishes include Rogan Josh, Dum Aloo, Modur Pulao, and Kahwa tea.

Dance & Music

Known for traditional dances like Rouf and music forms like Sufiyana Kalam.

Did You Know? Jammu and Kashmir has Asia’s largest tulip garden located in Srinagar.
